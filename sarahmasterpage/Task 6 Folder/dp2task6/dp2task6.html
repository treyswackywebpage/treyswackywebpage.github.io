Eye Color Alleles

The way that the genetics of eye color work is that the dominate trait show above the recessive.
Like how if a child has one brown eyed parent and one blue eyed the child is most likely to have brown eyes because it is a dominate trait about blue.
Although with different traits and alleles it is possible for the brwon eyed parent to be heterozygous(i.e. one brown eyed trait and one blue eyed trait) then
their is a small chance of the child having blue eyes.


Mendelian Charts

The way that we can track and predict the eye color of a child is using a mendelian chart.
The parents alleles are placed on the sides of the chart and they are crossed together. To predict
the chance/percentage of children that might have certain eye colors.
There are more than one trait that goes into eye color so in real life the actual chart is much larger than this example.
