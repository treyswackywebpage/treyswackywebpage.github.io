Biography(略歴)

I was born in Scottish Paisley textile trader's house. When I was 10 years old I lost my mother and when my father remarried, I quit the pastor 's school and in 1779 learned textile technology under my brother - in - law. After three years of practice, worked with textile craftsmen and so on, influenced by Scottish national poet Robert Barnes , he began to write poetry and works will be published in newspapers like Glasgow Advertiser became. The work became social and became a satire on the upper class. In 1790 I published a collection of works, but I did not get great success. Continuing efforts to succeed as a poet, the poetry of 1792, Watty and Meg or the Taming of a Shrew became the only widely known poetry collection. In 1783, a trial of defamation was brought up and it was impossible to pay a fine, so there was a case in which he had to incinerate the writings for a short period of time. Wilson decided to immigrate to the United States due to the failure of the women's problem.

In 1794 I went to nephew William Duncan and the American Philadelphia. I worked as a day labor, a hawker, a printing worker, a textile worker, and in 1796 I became a teacher of school in a milestone in Pennsylvania. I got a romance problem with a married woman and left that place in 1801. After that I also lived teaching in New Jersey and Pennsylvania. I met William Bertram , a naturalist who inherited the botanical garden from my father, and began to walk the way as a museum painter. At the beginning, I began drawing pictures of animals and trees, I became a successful businessman and met with an aviologist George Aard. By 1803 I began to travel for the observation of nature around the United States and planned to make a book of American birds. In 1806 he tried to hire Scottish acquaintance copperplate technicians but it did not come true due to lack of funds. From the summer of that year, he was hired by Bradford (Samuel F. Bradford) of the American version of the English encyclopedia (Rees' Cyclopedia) of Abraham Rees (Abraham Rees), the work of the production of the encyclopedia And succeeded in negotiating the publication of his bird books to Bradford.

Since 1807 I worked on " American Ornithology " work, investigated Pennsylvania and made illustrations in Philadelphia. Volume 1 was published in " American Ornithology " in September of 1808 . 200 copies were printed as a book of 158 pages with 34 hand coloring illustrations. Not only illustrations but also texts were written by Wilson and hand coloring himself. At the time, from the intention to develop the field of publication of natural science, which was thought to be late compared with Europe, materials such as paper were also used for paper and other materials. The purchase price of all 9 volumes was 120 dollars, which exceeded the annual income of teacher Wilson, but he traveled a journey to find purchasers and collected 250 buyers. Worked for the publication of subsequent volumes and acquisition of purchasers and published up to 7 volumes by the year 1813, but damaged the health and was sick in Philadelphia. George Odd took over the publication of " American Ornithology " and Wilson 's brief biography in the last volume.

Since 1828, the publication of John James Audubon 's " American Birds " (" The Birds of America ") has led to a relatively low rating of books.
